Typography

Rinko Kawauchi's Edge of the Everyday World Exhibition

I enjoyed learning about different typographic systems, and I like how Illustrator works.

A lot of my pieces are inspired by her photographs, and determine key shapes and colours.


Axial

Axial works by anchoring sides of text according to straight lines. I went for a corner of a printed photograph appearance. I chose an earthy turquoise for the main collection on exhibition, M/E (Mother Earth).

Lines of text emit from a focus, like rays from the sun. I shaped mine after a lense flare and kept some key words in them. This piece took the longest but was well worth it.

Radial

Dialational

Words bend and rotate around a focus, like ripples from a drop of water or like layers of an onion. Mine are inspired from the first example, I imagine it as a pond during soft rain.

A very jumbled composition that doesn't follow any specific rule in particular. I had fun stacking and using arrows, I tried to keep it legible but also varied orientations.

Random

Grid

Clusters of text are aligned to intersecting lines of the x or y axis. I modelled my work to look like a leaf with its veins. I used align to emphasise some key words.

Parts of text are put into individual groups but unlike grid don't have to be aligned. I contained mine in water droplets. The font makes it look soft despite the tails on the letters, almost retro and cartoony.

Modular

Transitional

Sentences or words sit in lines that mimic movement, of air, of liquid and so on. I was inspired in particular by a couple photos in Kawauchi's Ametsuchi collection which shows a mountain burning.

A straight axis is picked, and then words must be centered on it to make a symmetrical shape. I was inspired by a crystal in her Illuminance collection.
